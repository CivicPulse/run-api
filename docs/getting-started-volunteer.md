## Overview

CivicPulse Run is a mobile-friendly platform that helps you be effective in the field. Whether you are knocking on doors or making phone calls, the app guides you through each contact with scripts, navigation, and easy data entry.

**What you can do:**

- View and navigate your assigned walk lists for door-to-door canvassing
- Join phone bank sessions and make scripted voter calls
- Sign up for volunteer shifts
- Log your volunteer hours

The app is designed to work well on your phone -- you can do everything from a mobile browser.

## Getting Started

### 1. Get your registration link

Your campaign manager will share a registration link with you. This might come via email, text message, a social media post, or your campaign's website.

### 2. Create your account

1. Click the registration link
2. You will be taken to the ZITADEL login page
3. Click **Register** to create a new account
4. Fill in your name, email, and choose a password
5. Verify your email if prompted

### 3. Access the campaign

After registration, you will be redirected to CivicPulse Run and automatically connected to your campaign. If you were invited to multiple campaigns, you can switch between them from the home screen.

## Your Dashboard

After logging in, you will see your volunteer home view with:

- **Your campaigns** -- If you are a member of multiple campaigns, select which one to work on
- **Upcoming shifts** -- Any shifts you have signed up for
- **Active assignments** -- Walk lists or phone bank sessions assigned to you
- **Your stats** -- Doors knocked, calls made, hours logged

## Field Mode: Canvassing

Canvassing is door-to-door voter contact. Field Mode is designed for use on your phone while you are out walking.

### Accessing your walk list

1. Navigate to **Field** > **Canvassing**
2. Select your assigned walk list
3. You will see a list of addresses to visit, in walking order

### At each door

1. **Navigate** -- Tap the address to open directions in Google Maps (uses the street address for accurate navigation)
2. **Record the contact attempt** -- When you arrive at the door:
   - **Contact made** -- The voter answered; proceed to the survey
   - **Not home** -- No one answered
   - **Refused** -- Voter declined to speak
   - **Other** -- Moved, inaccessible, etc.
3. **Complete the survey** -- If the voter is available, follow the survey script on screen. Tap through each question and record their responses.
4. **Add notes** -- Optionally add any relevant notes about the interaction
5. **Move to next door** -- The app advances to the next address on your walk list

### Offline support

CivicPulse Run includes offline support for areas with poor cell connectivity:

- Survey data is saved locally on your device if you lose connection
- Data syncs automatically when connectivity returns
- You can continue working through your walk list even without signal

### Tracking your progress

The walk list view shows:
- Total doors on your list
- Doors completed
- Doors remaining
- Your contact rate (percentage of doors where you made contact)

## Field Mode: Phone Banking

Phone banking lets you make voter contact calls from anywhere -- home, a campaign office, or a coffee shop.

### Joining a phone bank session

1. Navigate to **Field** > **Phone Banking**
2. Select an active phone bank session
3. The session screen shows:
   - The campaign's call script
   - The current voter to call
   - Their phone number and basic information

### Making calls

1. **Review the voter info** -- Check the voter's name and any previous contact notes
2. **Make the call** -- Call the displayed phone number from your phone
3. **Follow the script** -- The survey script is displayed on screen; read through it naturally
4. **Record the outcome**:
   - **Completed survey** -- Voter answered and completed the script
   - **Not home / voicemail** -- No answer
   - **Refused** -- Voter declined
   - **Wrong number** -- Number is incorrect
   - **Do Not Call** -- Voter requested to be removed from call lists
5. **Next contact** -- The app automatically loads the next voter from the call list

### DNC compliance

If a voter asks not to be called again, select **Do Not Call** as the outcome. This permanently flags their number and they will be excluded from all future call lists. This is required by law -- always honor these requests.

## Shifts and Hours

### Viewing available shifts

1. Navigate to **Shifts** in your campaign
2. Browse upcoming shift slots showing:
   - Date and time
   - Location (e.g., "Campaign HQ", "Ward 3 staging area")
   - Activity type (canvassing, phone banking, office)
   - Available spots remaining

### Signing up

1. Find a shift that works for your schedule
2. Click **Sign Up**
3. The shift appears in your upcoming shifts on your dashboard

### Logging hours

After completing volunteer work:

1. Navigate to **Hours** in your campaign
2. Log the date, duration, and activity type
3. Your campaign manager can review and approve your logged hours

Your total volunteer hours are tracked and visible on your dashboard.

## Tips for Success

### Before heading out to canvass

- **Charge your phone fully** -- Field Mode is GPS-intensive; bring a portable charger if you have one
- **Download offline maps** -- If you are canvassing in an area with spotty cell service, download the area in Google Maps for offline navigation
- **Review the survey script** -- Read through the questions before your first door so the conversation flows naturally
- **Dress appropriately** -- Wear comfortable walking shoes and weather-appropriate clothing
- **Bring water** -- Canvassing shifts can be 2-3 hours of walking

### During canvassing

- **Be friendly and brief** -- Introduce yourself and the campaign, then move into the script
- **Listen actively** -- Voter responses are valuable data; record them accurately
- **Respect "no" answers** -- If someone does not want to talk, thank them and move on
- **Stay safe** -- Stick to your assigned route, canvass during daylight hours, and let someone know where you are

### For phone banking

- **Find a quiet space** -- Background noise makes calls harder for both you and the voter
- **Have water nearby** -- You will be talking a lot
- **Smile while you talk** -- It genuinely comes through in your voice
- **Take breaks** -- A 5-minute break every 30-45 minutes keeps you fresh
